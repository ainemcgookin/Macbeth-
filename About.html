About

For this year's spring production, a group of young actors and actresses pursued a supernatural take on the original operatic production, twisting the title of the play to "Macbet" in honor of the Italian Macbeth Opera production. While the original tragedy was written by Shakespeare and performed in 1606, it was adapted for the opera in Italy in 1847 by Giuseppe Verdi, Francesco Maria Piave, and Andrea Maffei, and the H in the name was dropped to fit the Italian pronunciation.

Inspired by cult classic movies like The Craft, The Shining, and The Good Witch, this production focuses on the supernatural themes in Macbeth's story, as well as the powerful role the female characters play.

For those new to the story, Macbet is a play about a powerless man twisted and controlled by fate and destiny. His adventures are plagued by control, death, and betrayal...
